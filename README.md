# Which New York Are You?

**Which New York Are You?** is a personality quiz that ends with something you can actually use: a six-day New York City itinerary built around the corners of the city that locals love and visitors miss.

Thirteen quick questions read your vibe: the soundtrack you'd choose for a midnight walk, your comfort food (a bodega chop cheese, or a bacon egg and cheese salt pepper ketchup), your low-key superpower, your fantasy job in old New York, and your appetite for splendor. Behind the scenes, your answers feed a deck of twelve elemental spirit guides, each one a real New York creature rendered as an ornate, Art Deco tarot card: Lumen the Firefly (Light), Noir the Bodega Cat (Shadow), Gale the Peregrine Falcon (Storm), Rivet the Subway Rat (Iron), Moxie the Harbor Seal (Tide), Luna the Moth in 7-train purple (Dream), and six more. Every hero is grounded in genuine city wildlife: the parrots of Green-Wood Cemetery, the salamanders of Inwood Hill Park, the snowy owl that once stopped Central Park in its tracks.

At the end, you draw two cards, a primary guide and a sidekick, and together they route your trip. The itinerary follows two firm rules: each day stays within one neighborhood, its stops in easy proximity, and each of the six days centers on a different neighborhood drawn from a pool of twenty-one across the boroughs, from Greenpoint, Red Hook, and Bay Ridge to Jackson Heights, the East Village, Koreatown, Harlem, and the Financial District. Every stop is a real place (morning walk, coffee, lunch, afternoon, dinner, and a nightcap) plus a detour and a dessert tuned to your spending mood (velvet-rope plush or hidden gem), favoring century-old counters, hidden parks, free galleries, and bars behind unmarked doors over anything with a gift shop. Each day ships with a one-tap Google Maps route through every stop, and a combined route covers the extras.

The deck also reads more than elements. Your answers trace your **sigils**, three behavioral signatures with names like Steady Beacon, Social Catalyst, and Kinetic Anchor: patterns in how you tend to move through a day, never labels on who you are. Each unfolds into a full reading with evidence, resonance, and tags, written to be neurodiversity-affirming throughout. Your leading sigil deals a hand of bonus stops tuned to it, from the Panorama at the Queens Museum to Carmine Street Guitars, alongside wildcards from your two heroes.

You can take the reading with you: export the itinerary as CSV, copy it formatted for docs, print it to PDF, or download both of your tarot cards in a single zip. Results live in the URL, so a reading can be shared or revisited.

Styled like a subway route map crossed with a tarot reading, the experience is mobile-first, meets WCAG AA color contrast (see `style-guide.html` for the palette and contrast ledger), uses honest accessible semantics on every accordion and dialog, and respects reduced-motion settings.
