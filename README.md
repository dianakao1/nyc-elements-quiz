# Which New York Are You?

**Which New York Are You?** is a personality quiz that ends with something you can actually use: a five-day New York City itinerary built around the corners of the city that locals love and visitors miss.

Eleven quick questions read your vibe — the soundtrack you'd choose for a midnight walk, the scent you'd bottle, your best-case Friday night, the weather that makes you happiest. Behind the scenes, your answers feed a deck of twelve elemental spirit guides, each one a real New York creature rendered as an ornate, Art Deco tarot card: Lumen the Firefly (Light), Noir the Bodega Cat (Shadow), Gale the Peregrine Falcon (Storm), Rivet the Subway Rat (Iron), Moxie the Harbor Seal (Tide), and eight more. Every hero is grounded in genuine city wildlife — the parrots of Green-Wood Cemetery, the salamanders of Inwood Hill Park, the snowy owl that once stopped Central Park in its tracks.

At the end, you draw two cards — a primary guide and a sidekick — and together they route your trip. The itinerary follows two firm rules: each day stays entirely within one walkable neighborhood, and each of the five days centers on a different neighborhood across the boroughs, from Greenpoint and Red Hook to Jackson Heights, Koreatown, Harlem, and the Financial District. Every stop is a real place — morning walk, coffee, lunch, afternoon, dinner, and a nightcap — favoring century-old counters, hidden parks, free galleries, and bars behind unmarked doors over anything with a gift shop.

The deck also reads more than elements. Two questions quietly divine your worldly calling — finance, engineering, design, data science, medicine, mathematics, or real estate — and deal three bonus stops tuned to it, from the Panorama at the Queens Museum to Hamilton's grave at Trinity Church.

The whole experience is a single HTML file: React and Tailwind loaded from CDNs, no build step, no backend, no tracking. Styled like a subway route map crossed with a tarot reading, it's mobile-first, respects reduced-motion settings, and is entirely remixable. Fork it, reskin the deck, or swap in your own city — the spirits won't mind.
